Norwegian Folk Museum

In the afternoon, we'll visit the Norsk Folk Museum, an open-air museum presenting 155 traditional houses from different regions of Norway. The museum offers numerous activities including folk dances, traditional pancake preparation, crafts, farm animals, staff in traditional costumes, and more.

Entry fee: £17 (€8.50 per person)

Our Oslo Adventure: Highlights

Cultural Experiences

Oslo offered us an incredible blend of historical and modern cultural experiences. From the ancient Akershus Fortress to the contemporary Opera House, from viewing "The Scream" at the National Museum to exploring the Norwegian Folk Museum's traditional houses.

Natural Beauty

The city's connection to nature was evident throughout our journey - from the serene Hovedøya Island with its beaches and monastery ruins to the artistic Ekeberg and Vigeland sculpture parks set in beautiful natural surroundings.

Culinary Discoveries

Norwegian cuisine surprised us with its variety and quality, from traditional dishes at Jimmy's to international options at the Street Food Hall and pizza at Winter Frognerparker.

Oslo's Atmosphere

What stood out most was Oslo's unique atmosphere - a perfect balance between a vibrant capital city and one of Europe's greenest urban centers. The city's commitment to accessibility, cleanliness, and preserving its natural surroundings makes it a model for sustainable urban living.

Our six days in Oslo gave us just a taste of what Norway has to offer, combining natural beauty, rich history, modern design, and warm hospitality into an unforgettable experience.
